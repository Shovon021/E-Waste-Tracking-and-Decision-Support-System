## 2. Abstract

Electronic waste (e-waste) management is a critical challenge for large corporate and educational organizations. This project introduces a high-capacity, web-based **E-Waste Asset Tracking & Decision Support Dashboard** designed for large-scale operations. Featuring an enterprise-grade light theme, the application is pre-populated with a realistic corporate dataset of **1,050 hardware records** across 7 product categories and 7 departments of a multinational corporation. 

The dashboard provides a complete data management interface, enabling users to:
1. **Upload custom CSV datasets** (such as files downloaded from Kaggle, ServiceNow, or asset management systems) which are parsed dynamically using *PapaParse*.
2. **Manually add hardware assets** in real-time.
3. **Analyze disposal pathways** (Reuse, Repair, Recycle, Safe Disposal) generated by a rule-based decision support logic.

All environmental metrics, cost reductions, carbon offset stats, and Chart.js visualizations update dynamically. Calculations are calibrated with national utility standards, such as Bangladesh Power Development Board (BPDB) industrial rates of ৳8.19/kWh and grid emission factors of 0.59 kg CO₂/kWh, demonstrating operational energy cost reductions, manufacturing energy savings (embodied carbon), and high landfill diversion rates.

## 3. Introduction

### Background of the Sustainability Problem
Corporate and educational organizations purchase computers, laptops, routers, and batteries in bulk. When these items reach the end of their operational cycles, they are decommissioned. Due to a lack of unified auditing systems, obsolete hardware often accumulates in storage facilities, gets disposed of in standard municipal landfills, or is outsourced to uncertified recyclers. This creates significant environmental degradation: toxic lead, mercury, and chemical leaks, alongside the lost opportunity to reclaim valuable copper, gold, and rare earth elements.

### Importance from Computing and Environmental Perspectives
* **Computing Perspective:** By implementing local-first web processing, parsing engines, and logical decision structures, organizations can convert raw, disjointed spreadsheet data into live visual analytics, facilitating rapid audit compliance and inventory decisions.
* **Environmental Perspective:** Extending the lifecycle of a computer by 3 years through repair and internal redeployment directly avoids the high carbon emissions and water usage associated with fabricating new microprocessors. Conserving this "embodied energy" yields much higher environmental benefits than basic raw material recycling.

## 4. Problem Statement

### Selected Problem
Large institutions lack a centralized, dynamic dashboard to monitor operational hardware lifecycles, estimate annual electrical load overheads of legacy fleets, assess equipment conditions, and classify disposal pathways. Ad-hoc decommissioning results in:
* Premature disposal of easily repairable hardware.
* Continued operation of highly inefficient legacy servers and monitors.
* Inability to measure or report ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) sustainability metrics to administration.

### Stakeholders and System Boundary
* **System Boundary:** The registry accepts hardware inventory data spanning laptops, desktops, monitors, peripherals, networking equipment, storage drives, and corporate UPS battery units.
* **Key Stakeholders:**
  * **IT Asset Managers:** Responsible for inputting inventory items and assessing condition scores.
  * **Corporate Sustainability Directors:** Audit the carbon emissions avoided and track landfill diversion rates for institutional ESG reporting.
  * **Procurement Departments:** Evaluate cost-saving margins to allocate hardware upgrade budgets.
  * **Certified Recycling Partners:** Safely handle items labeled for recycling and hazard containment.

## 5. Assumptions and Data Source

### Assumptions
The system's impact calculation engine runs on the following parameters:
* **Grid Emission Factor (EF):** 0.59 kg CO₂/kWh (calibrated to the Bangladesh natural gas-dominant power grid mix).
* **Electricity Cost Rate (R):** ৳8.19/kWh (BPDB industrial tariff baseline).
* **Operational Cycle Time (T):** 8 hours/day × 280 working days/year = 2,240 annual hours.
* **Operational Energy Reduction:** Repaired hardware achieves a 10% efficiency boost due to formatting/thermal cleaning. Replaced devices (Recycled/Disposed) are modeled to consume 40% less power by upgrading to ENERGY STAR modern equivalents.
* **Manufacturing Energy (Embodied Energy):** Sourced from average lifecycle assessments (LCA):
  * Computer: 1,200 kWh
  * Monitor: 750 kWh
  * Networking: 300 kWh
  * Peripherals: 200 kWh
  * Storage/Mobile: 100 kWh

### Data Source
To prove scalability, a realistic large-enterprise dataset consisting of **1,050 individual asset records** was generated. It includes fields for purchase year, weight, brand, department, and operational power draw. The application can accept any external CSV file containing these columns, instantly recalculating all calculations upon upload.

## 6. System Design

### System Architecture
The application runs as a local-first single page web dashboard:
* **Storage Layer (`data.js`):** A pre-loaded array of 1,050 asset objects.
* **Parser Layer (PapaParse):** Reads client-uploaded CSV files and normalizes column headers.
* **Logic Engines:**
  * `decision-engine.js`: Classifies condition scores into classes and maps them to recommendations.
  * `calculations.js`: Evaluates baseline operational loads against optimized workloads.
* **Visual Layer (`charts.js` & `index.html`):** Renders responsive Chart.js components, summary badges, and filterable tables.

## 7. Methodology

### Decision Support Logic
The system implements a decision matrix using age, condition score (1–10), replacement cost, and hazardous properties:

```
IF condition_score <= 1 OR contains_hazardous = True
   → DISPOSE (Certify safe hazardous waste routing)
   
IF condition_score >= 7
   → REUSE (Redeploy internally or donate)
   
IF condition_score between 4 and 6 (Fair)
   IF estimated_repair_cost < (0.4 × replacement_cost)
      → REPAIR
   ELSE
      → RECYCLE (Deconstruct for raw metals)
      
IF condition_score between 2 and 3 (Poor)
   → RECYCLE
```

### Analytical Formulas
1. **Operational Load (kWh):** 
   $$E = \frac{N \times P \times T}{1,000}$$
2. **Electricity Expense (৳):** 
   $$Cost = E \times R$$
3. **Grid Emissions (kg CO₂):** 
   $$CO_2 = E \times EF$$
4. **Conservation Margins (%):** 
   $$Saving(\%) = \frac{\text{Before} - \text{After}}{\text{Before}} \times 100$$

## 9. Results and Evaluation

Upon loading the default 1,050-item dataset, the dashboard computes the following results:
* **Fleet Volume:** 1,050 items spanning desktop towers, laptops, enterprise switches, and UPS batteries.
* **Baseline Load vs. Optimization:** Shows an annual energy consumption decrease of approximately 35–40% by replacing obsolete, high-wattage hardware with modern energy-efficient items, combined with extending the lifecycles of good/fair items.
* **Cost Efficiency:** Demonstrates annual utility bill savings of hundreds of thousands of BDT.
* **Landfill Avoidance:** Achieves a landfill diversion rate of over 85% by classifying the majority of obsolete items into Reuse, Repair, and Raw Material Recycling pathways.

## 10. Deployment Considerations

* **Feasibility:** Fully client-side processing removes the need for web application servers, backend databases, or cloud infrastructure, enabling rapid offline distribution.
* **Data Security & Integrity:** Local parsing ensures that sensitive corporate hardware serial numbers, models, and departmental structures never leave the client machine.
* **Integration Capacity:** The uploader is designed to parse standard exports from enterprise inventory databases (e.g., CSV files from Kaggle or company asset sheets).
* **Limitations:** The accuracy of the sustainability output relies entirely on correct manual assessments of device condition scores and device power ratings.
